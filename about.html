About

Ingrid Aszkenasy is a contemporary textile artist whose work explores the boundaries between traditional craft and modern artistic expression. With a background in fine arts and a passion for textile techniques, Ingrid creates pieces that blend intricate handwork with conceptual depth.

Her artistic practice centers on the exploration of texture, color relationships, and the narrative potential of materials. Each piece is carefully constructed, often incorporating found objects, natural fibers, and experimental techniques that challenge conventional notions of textile art.

Education & Background

Ingrid studied Fine Arts with a specialization in Textile Arts, developing a unique approach that combines traditional embroidery and weaving techniques with contemporary mixed-media practices. Her work has been influenced by both classical textile traditions and modern conceptual art movements.

Artistic Philosophy

At the heart of Ingrid's work is a belief in the power of slow, deliberate creation. In an age of digital immediacy, her handcrafted pieces invite viewers to pause and engage with the materiality of the work. Each stitch, each thread, carries intention and meaning, creating a dialogue between the artist, the material, and the viewer.

Her pieces often explore themes of memory, transformation, and the relationship between the natural and constructed worlds. Through her work, Ingrid seeks to create spaces for contemplation and connection.

Exhibitions & Recognition

Ingrid's work has been featured in numerous group exhibitions and private collections. She continues to develop her practice while maintaining a commitment to the craft traditions that inform her contemporary vision.
